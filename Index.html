2019

Evolving into a next-generation event organiser

The Group acquires Shoptalk and Groceryshop, two best-in-class products focused on e-commerce for the retail, grocery and consumer packaged goods industries.

2019

New business, new brand

ITE rebrands to become Hyve, reflecting the evolution of the Group throughout the TAG programme and moving it into the future, with a clear vision, well- defined strategy and a renewed energy. The Group reports like-for-like revenue and profit growth and strong margin improvement, reflecting the transformation undergone during the TAG programme.

2018

Another market-leading acquisition

Mining Indaba joins Africa Oil Week in the Group’s Global brands division. The event is recognised by its customers as the ‘must-attend’ event for the mining sector, attracting key participants in the African mining value chain.

2018

Move to vibrant new HQ

The Group develops and rolls out best practice in each area of event organisation and moves to new open and modern headquarters in Paddington Central, London.

2018

Changing shape

The Group’s portfolio changes significantly with the acquisition of market-leading events Bett, CWIEME, Spring Fair, Autumn Fair, Pure London and Glee.

2017

Becoming the best

The Group announces a new vision and the start of its Transformation and Growth (TAG) programme, the largest ever investment into its products and processes, with a clear focus on running must attend shows in strong sectors wherever they are in the world.

2016

Broadening presence in China

The Group acquires a 70% stake in ITE Gehua Exhibitions Co Ltd to run a number of exhibitions in China, chief amongst them being World Seafood Shanghai.

2016

A new era begins

Mark Shashoua returns to the Group, ushering in a new management team and kicking of a strategic review of the business.

2015

Powering into the global energy sector

The acquisition of a majority stake in a worldwide conference portfolio which includes Africa Oil Week further strengthens the Group’s Oil&Gas portfolio and enables further geographic diversification.

2014

A big move into a new sector

ITE moves into another strategically important industry sector with the acquisition of the Breakbulk series of events serving the transport and logistics market for large-scale project equipment. The Group further expands its business in Southeast Asia by joining forces with Debindo, the owners of the Indobuildtech series of events in Indonesia.

2012

Start of a new partnership

ITE acquires a minority shareholding in the Indian exhibition organiser, Asian Business Exhibitions & Conferences Ltd (ABEC), one of India’s leading exhibition businesses, running exhibitions in markets including construction, architecture, design, education, lifestyle, real estate, and oil & gas.

2011

Strengthening the building portfolio

ITE acquires a 60% stake in YEMF in Turkey, the organiser of TurkeyBuild, making the building and interiors portfolio the largest in the group.

2010

More flagship events join the family

ITE acquires Paperex, the International Exhibition & Conference on Pulp, Paper & Allied Industries in India, and the organiser of Aquatherm events in Ukraine, Kazakhstan and Azerbaijan.

2006

Spreading eastwards

ITE moves into another new territory by opening its first office in Beijing, China. Today Hyve’s Beijing office continues to play a pivotal role by connecting Chinese exhibitors with its market-leading events worldwide.

2002

New look for a growing business

A new ITE brand and orange and grey logo is introduced, which will remain the Group’s identity until it rebrands to Hyve in 2019.

2001

Best foot forward

Moda, the heart of UK fashion, joins the ITE family offering a curated and comprehensive selection of womenswear, accessories and footwear, from both leading brands and exciting new names. Celebrated as the National Footwear Show, Moda is also the leading footwear trade show in the UK.

2000

A new acquisition bears fruit

The Group acquires WorldFood Moscow, a major exhibition serving the global food and drinks industry. The event has since grown to become the entry point for international manufacturers looking to expand into the vibrant Russian market.

1998

Leading the way

ITE becomes the first pure-play exhibition organiser and one of the first emerging market businesses not in oil and gas or mining to list on the London Stock Exchange. The Group headquarters also moves to new offices in Queens Park, London.

1996

Moving onwards and upwards

The Group moves into transport and logistics services and technologies with the launch of TransRussia in Moscow, still the largest event of its kind in Russia.

1995

A star is born

Russia’s largest building and interiors trade event, Mosbuild, opens its doors in Moscow for the first time in 1995. Today, the event showcases around 1,200 Russian and international exhibitors from across the building and interiors industry and welcomes over 75,000 visitors.

1994

Exploring new sectors and markets

The Group moves into build and interiors with the launch of KazBuild in Almaty and launches the first international travel and tourism show in Moscow (MITT). The Group also sets up a subsidiary – ITECA - to run events in Central Asia.

1993

Breaking new ground

After just 12 months of preparation, ITE organises the 1st Moscow International Oil & Gas Exhibition & Conference (MIOGE) attended by over 400 companies representing 22 countries in August 1992. The 1st Kazakhstan International Oil & Gas Exhibition & Conference (KIOGE) follows in October 1993.

1992

Early success stories

As new markets continue to open up, ITE becomes the first to plan, organise and successfully manage the 1st Moscow International Motor Show in August 1992, which was followed by other motor shows in Prague and Bucharest. Over one million trade and public visitors visit the Group’s early international motor shows and over 1,000 members of the media attend press launches resulting in extensive press and broadcast coverage.

1991

Our pioneering journey begins

The Shashoua family identifies an opportunity to provide global brands with a chance to explore the early potential in newly emerging markets and starts ITE as an organiser of global trade exhibitions and conferences. Its first event, the 1st International Prague Book Fair and Writers’ Festival, is well received and attended by President Havel, President of the Czech Republic.
